2025-2026 Bill 5379 Text of Previous Version (Mar. 24, 2026) - South Carolina Legislature Online South Carolina General Assembly126th Session, 2025-2026Bill 5379Indicates Matter StrickenIndicates New Matter(Text matches printed bills. Document has been reformatted to meet World Wide Web specifications.) A house RESOLUTION TO CONGRATULATE AMBROSE "RICKY" WILSON JR. ON THE OCCASION OF HIS SEVENTIETH BIRTHDAY AND TO WISH HIM A JOYOUS BIRTHDAY CELEBRATION AND MANY YEARS OF CONTINUED HEALTH AND HAPPINESS. Whereas, the members of the South Carolina House of Representatives are delighted to learn that Ambrose "Ricky" Wilson Jr. will be honored at a seventieth birthday celebration on Saturday, March 28, 2026; and Whereas, Mr. Wilson was born on March 25, 1956, in Georgetown to the late Ambrose Wilson Sr. and Helen G. Wilson. He was reared in a loving family with six siblings: the late Willie Mae Bryant, the late Priscilla Evans, Mary Helen, Debra, Undria, and Keddrick. He first learned carpentry and brick masonry skills alongside his father; and Whereas, he attended J.B. Beck Elementary School and graduated from Howard High School in Georgetown. After high school, he continued his education at Denmark Technical College where he further developed carpentry and brick masonry skills, which would become the foundation of a lifetime of craftsmanship and service to others; and Whereas, after graduating from Denmark Technical College, Mr. Wilson began a long and dedicated career with the Charleston County School District. He served at Lincoln High School for more than thirty-seven years until the school closed in 2016. During his time at Lincoln, he taught carpentry and coached both football and basketball, positively influencing and mentoring countless students along the way; and Whereas, following the closing of Lincoln High School, he continued his work with Charleston County Schools as a maintenance supervisor and a teacher at Burke High School. In 2017, he transitioned to the Georgetown County School District, where he currently teaches carpentry at Georgetown High School, continuing to share his knowledge and passion with the next generation; and Whereas, for more than forty years, Mr. Wilson has worked as a contractor known for his exceptional skill and craftsmanship. He has had a remarkable ability to build or repair almost anything; and Whereas, he has faithfully served his church, Bethesda, for many years. He has been a trustee for more than twenty years and chairman of the trustee ministry and finance committee for more than ten years. He is always willing to go above and beyond wherever he is needed. It is his responsibility to ensure that the sanctuary is physically equipped for all services, funerals, and programs and that the fellowship hall and kitchen are in good standing for meetings, repast, and fellowship. He ushers whenever he is needed; and Whereas, Mr. Wilson is an avid and successful gardener. He grows a variety of fresh vegetables every year such as tomatoes, corn, green beans and collard greens. Whatever produce he does not give to family and friends, he cans and freezes; and Whereas, God has truly blessed Mr. Wilson, and the South Carolina House of Representatives is grateful for his dedication to his family, his community, his church, and the many lives he has impacted throughout the years. His legacy of hard work, service, and generosity continues to inspire all who know him. Now, therefore, Be it resolved by the House of Representatives: That the members of the South Carolina House of Representatives, by this resolution, congratulate Ambrose "Ricky" Wilson Jr. on the occasion of his seventieth birthday and wish him a joyous birthday celebration and many years of continued health and happiness. Be it further resolved that a copy of this resolution be presented to Ambrose "Ricky" Wilson Jr. ----XX---- This web page was last updated on March 24, 2026 at 01:12 PM
