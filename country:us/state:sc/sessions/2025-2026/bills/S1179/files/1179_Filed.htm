A senate RESOLUTION

 

TO RECOGNIZE AND HONOR Jack Logan During National Nurses Week AND TO Commend him for his service in Healthcare and for his work to promote gun Safety.

 

Whereas, the members of the South Carolina Senate are pleased to recognize Jack Logan during National Nurses Week; and

 

Whereas, a native of Greenville, Mr. Logan is a graduate of Cowpens High School, now known as Broome High School. He also attended Greenville Technical College and the University of South Carolina; and

 

Whereas, Mr. Logan began his career in healthcare as a certified nursing assistant at Spartanburg Convalescent in 1990. He has since worked throughout the Upstate for many nursing homes, hospitals, and nursing registries serving private homes. He currently works as Springbrook Behavioral Health in Travelers Rest; and

 

Whereas, a civic leader, Mr. Logan has served with many community organizations, including as the founder of Put Down the Guns Now Young People. A national partner of Project Childsafe and the National Shooting Sports Foundation, this organization teaches young individuals about gun safety through gun safety events, firearms safety materials, and the distribution of gun locks, which helps reduce the number of gun accidents. Since its founding in 2010, Mr. Logan has overseen the distribution of more than twenty-five thousand gun locks throughout the State; and

 

Whereas, the members of the South Carolina Senate greatly appreciate the dedication and commitment that Mr. Logan has shown in serving the people and the State of South Carolina.  Now, therefore,

 

Be it resolved by the Senate:

 

That the members of the South Carolina Senate, by this resolution, recognize and honor Jack Logan during National Nurses Week and commend him for his service in healthcare and for his work to promote gun safety.

 

Be it further resolved that a copy of this resolution be presented to Jack Logan.
