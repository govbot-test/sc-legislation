2025-2026 Bill 3003 Text of Previous Version (Jan. 30, 2025) - South Carolina Legislature Online South Carolina General Assembly126th Session, 2025-2026(Text matches printed bills. Document has been reformatted to meet World Wide Web specifications.) Committee Report January 30, 2025 H. 3003 Introduced by Reps. Collins and Beach S. Printed 1/30/25--H. Read the first time January 14, 2025 ________ The committee on House Invitations and Memorial Resolutions To whom was referred a Concurrent Resolution (H. 3003) to request the Department of Transportation name the portion of Main Street in the Town of Easley in Pickens County from Wilbur Street to North Pendleton Street, etc., respectfully Report: That they have duly and carefully considered the same, and recommend that the same do pass: DENNIS MOSS for Committee. _______ A concurrent RESOLUTION to request the Department of Transportation name the portion of Main Street in the Town of Easley in pickens County from Wilbur Street to North Pendleton Street "Officer Matthew Logan Hare Memorial avenue." Whereas, Officer Matthew Logan Hare was born on February 3, 2001; and Whereas, a 2019 graduate of Easley High School, Matthew began his career in the field of law enforcement in 2022 when he enrolled in the South Carolina Criminal Justice Academy and acquired the skills and knowledge necessary to excel as a law enforcement officer; and Whereas, upon graduating from the Criminal Justice Academy, he became an officer with the Easley Police Department; and Whereas, on August 2, 2023, at the age of twenty-two, and just five months after graduating from the Criminal Justice Academy, Officer Matthew Hare was struck and killed in the line of duty by an Amtrak train while attempting to save a woman who was threatening to harm herself on the railroad tracks along East Main Street in Easley; and Whereas, because of his bravery and heroism, in 2024, the South Carolina Department of Public Safety inducted Officer Hare into the South Carolina Law Enforcement Hall of Fame; and Whereas, to honor Officer Hare's life and commemorate his unwavering commitment to duty, it would be fitting and proper to name a portion of Main Street in the Town of Easley as a memorial to this son of the Palmetto State. Now, therefore, Be it resolved by the House of Representatives, the Senate concurring: That the members of the South Carolina General Assembly, by this resolution, request the Department of Transportation name the portion of Main Street in the Town of Easley in Pickens County from Wilbur Street to North Pendleton Street "Officer Matthew Logan Hare Memorial Avenue" and erect appropriate signs or markers along this portion of highway containing these words. Be it further resolved that a copy of this resolution be forwarded to the Department of Transportation. ----XX--- This web page was last updated on January 30, 2025 at 02:43 PM
