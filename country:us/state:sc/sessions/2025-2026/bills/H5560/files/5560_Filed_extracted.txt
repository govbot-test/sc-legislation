Whereas, the members of the South Carolina House of Representatives were pleased to hear that the Military Magnet Academy girls basketball team took home the highest honor at the 2026 Class A State Championship game held on Saturday, March 7, at Colonial Life Arena; and Whereas, this championship game showcased the hard work and determination that this team has put in all year. These young athletes, including two seventh graders, two eighth graders, and four freshmen, defied all of the odds stacked against them. The Military Magnet Academy Eagles dominated their competition, Great Falls High School, from the beginning of the game. The Eagles forced thirty-one turnovers and twenty-three steals. The team also had nine three-pointers and shot forty-five percent from the floor. The Eagles ended the game 70-21, finishing the season with an impressive 26-4 record and winning back-to-back championship titles; and Whereas, Military Magnet Academy had many standout performances at the championship game, including Mariah Brown, who scored twenty-five points, including five three-pointers and seven rebounds, Da'Nariyah Williams, who scored ten points and grabbed eight boards, and Kaleah Brown-King, who scored eleven points and seven rebounds; and Whereas, this win marks the Military Magnet Academy girls basketball team's fifth championship win in the last six years; and Whereas, Head Coach Nathaniel Taylor and his talented coaching staff utilized their own experience and passion for the sport to craft a championship-caliber team. The lessons that they have taught these young athletes will continue to impact them on and off the court; and Whereas, the Military Magnet Academy girls basketball team has brought immense pride and recognition to their school and community. The members of the South Carolina House of Representatives are honored to recognize these outstanding student athletes for their achievements both on the court and in the classroom. They look forward to hearing of the team's future accomplishments in the days to come. Now, therefore, Be it resolved by the House of Representatives: That the members of the South Carolina House of Representatives, by this resolution, honor and recognize the Military Magnet Academy girls basketball team, coaches, and school officials for an outstanding season and congratulate them on winning the 2026 South Carolina Class A State Championship title. Be it further resolved that a copy of this resolution be presented to Military Magnet Academy Principal Dr. Robert L. Perrineau Jr. and Head Coach Nathaniel Taylor. ----XX---- This web page was last updated on April 22, 2026 at 10:54 AM
