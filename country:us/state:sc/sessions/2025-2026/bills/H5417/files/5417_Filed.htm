A house RESOLUTION

 

to remember and celebrate the life of Dale Johnson Moore and to extend the deepest sympathy to her large and loving family and many friends.

 

Whereas, the members of the South Carolina House of Representatives were deeply saddened to learn of the passing of Dale Johnson Moore on Monday, March 16, 2026; and

 

Whereas, born in Augusta, Georgia to parents Eddie and Doris Williams Johnson on November 9, 1951; and

 

Whereas, she was the devoted and beloved wife of Mr. Tommy Moore for fifty-five years, sharing with him a life marked by steadfast love, partnership, and companionship; and

 

Whereas, a dedicated civic leader, Mrs. Moore was a constant and cheerful presence in the Clearwater community, most notably through her involvement with Valley Recreation, where she generously gave her time and could always be found on the sidelines, enthusiastically supporting not only her own children but countless others; and

 

Whereas, a devout Christian, she was a member of Christian Heritage Church in Graniteville. Her life was a reflection of her deep faith and her belief that every child is a gift from God, a sentiment beautifully captured on a plaque at Helping Hands in Aiken. She did not just speak those words, she lived them daily, pouring her heart into being a loving and supportive mama and grandma; and

 

Whereas, Mrs. Moore had a gift for bringing smiles to those around her. Her humor was effortless, and her laughter unforgettable. Quiet in nature but abundant in generosity, she gave selflessly and loved deeply. She embodied the Fruit of the Spirit - love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control - and her life stands as a lasting and powerful testament to those virtues; and

 

Whereas, Mrs. Moore was preceded in death by her parents and her brother, Dickie Johnson; and

 

Whereas, Mrs. Moore leaves to cherish her memory her loving husband Tommy; her sons and daughters-in-law, Baylen and Donna Moore, and Brent and Kristi Moore; her brother, Alvin Johnson; her beloved cousin, Becky Watts; and her treasured grandchildren, Luke, Rhett, Spencer, Bennett, Grayson, and Lindsey, each of whom brought her immeasurable joy and pride; and

 

Whereas, the members of the South Carolina House of Representatives are grateful for the life and legacy of Mrs. Dale Johnson Moore and for the example of kindness and service to others that she set for all who knew her. Now, therefore,

 

Be it resolved by the House of Representatives:

 

That the members of the South Carolina House of Representatives, by this resolution, remember and celebrate the life of Dale Johnson Moore and extend the deepest sympathy to her large and loving family and many friends.

 

Be it further resolved that a copy of this resolution be presented to the family of Dale Johnson Moore.
